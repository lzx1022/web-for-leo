POTICO ATTAVIA MARKET
Rome, Italy

Prof. Frank Martinez & Prof. Michael Graves

This urban design proposal initiates with a reflection on the urban site and how that process determines the quality of site knowledge; interpretations of Rome’s past facilitate projections of its future. The studio operates as a form of design-based critical inquiry. Through our research, we emphasize a critical reassessment of the historic urban site relative to questions of program, infrastructure, and cultural changes aided by the development of architectural form. The generation of formal and programmatic approaches lead to the design of new urban and architectural presences. Potico Attavia Market responds to the new urban conditions the city now faces while underlining its significant historic heritage. Our emphasis is given to the analysis of architectural and urban layers as related to changes and transformations over time.
