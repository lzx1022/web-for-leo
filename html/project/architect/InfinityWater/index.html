Prof. Armando Montero

Architecture Centers vary greatly in size and complexity, generally comprising a number of distinct but overlapping roles. Perhaps first and foremost, they are places for architects to meet and share information. They are also places for tourists to become oriented, and for residents and professional to explore the built environment. Further, they serve as urban laboratories, creative platforms for civic activism. Many serve other roles, like maintaining archives, public reading romms and bookstores. At the beginning of the 21st century the role of Architecture Centers continues to evolve, a response not only changes in urban and architectural culture, but also in response to the transformative role of new media and internet culture.
